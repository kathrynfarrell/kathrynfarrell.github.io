§ 3: Ongoing Projects

Attention Schemas in Artificial Neural Networks

Advisor: Dr. Michael Graziano
What happens when we implement social cognition theories for attention modulation into deep learning models? Here, we modify transformer-based neural network architectures to examine self-modeling attention mechanisms and their impact on multiagent cooperation.

Understanding the Latent Structure of Population Responses in the Hippocampus

Advisor: Dr. Jonathan Pillow
Studies suggest that the behavior of "non-place" cells in the hippocampus can be predicted from the activity of their neighbors, and that population responses in the hippocampal cell network can be well described by a location-blind maximum entropy model. Here we examine the potential information encoded by non-place cells, and by the collective state of the hippocampal network, using Gaussian Process models.

Describing Stimulated Cortical Circuits with Dynamical Similarity Analysis (DSA) and Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD)

Advisor: Dr. Azadeh Yazdan
How are the dynamics of cortical circuits changed by optogenetic stimulation experiments, and how can we map the properties of dynamical system models onto meaningful features of the original cortical circuits? Here, we analyze networks in primate sensorimotor cortex as linear dynamical systems, and demonstrate a sustained distortion of these systems when optogenetic stimulations are applied over time. We are currently developing computational analyses for interpreting the evolution of dynamic modes through the lens of firing frequencies and spatial correlations within the cortical circuits.
